# Executive Summary: Pakistan Tech Compensation Audit

**The question:** Do Pakistan-based developers earn less than developers elsewhere, and does 
that gap hold up once you account for experience — or is it just because Pakistan's respondents 
tend to be less experienced?

**The finding:** Pakistan-based developers report a median salary of **$8,428/year**, compared 
to **$78,000/year** globally. Even after accounting for experience, Rest-of-World compensation is 
roughly **5.3x** higher — confirmed by two separate statistical methods that agree with each other.

**What this does NOT mean:** This is not proof of discrimination. It's proof the gap isn't simply 
explained by developers in Pakistan being less experienced. Company size, job role, remote-work 
status, and cost-of-living differences were not measured here and likely account for a meaningful 
share of the remaining gap.

## Three Things to Take Away

1. **The gap is real and it's large** — not a statistical fluke of a big sample size, but a 
   substantively significant difference by standard benchmarks.
2. **Experience alone doesn't explain it** — the gap shows up at every experience level tested, 
   from entry-level to senior.
3. **This is a signal to investigate further, not a final verdict** — a proper audit would need 
   role, company size, and cost-of-living data before anyone could responsibly say more.

## Recommended Next Steps

- Expand the analysis to include job role, seniority level, and company size
- Adjust for purchasing power (cost of living), not just currency conversion
- Treat this as a starting point for a deeper compensation study, not a standalone conclusion
